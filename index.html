Hi! I'm Kaitlyn (Kait) Shin (she/her/hers).

Currently, I'm a 4th year PhD candidate and NSF Graduate Research Fellow at MIT Astrophysics. I work with Prof. Kiyoshi Masui and the CHIME/FRB Collaboration to learn all about Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs).
Site is under construction!

Research

I think a lot about FRB population questions -- e.g., where do they reside, how are their brightnesses distributed, how many populations of FRBs might there be? -- while endeavoring to correct for observational selection effects as best as possible.
I contributed significantly to the CHIME/FRB Catalog 1 efforts, and am actively working on internal infrastructure to carefully analyze larger numbers of events towards future catalog efforts as well.
I also use the Magellan telescope to characterize host galaxy candidates of FRBs.

Outreach and Service

Throughout grad school I've been involved with Astrobites, Astronomy on Tap @Boston/Cambridge, MIT Astrogazers, MOSTEC, MSRP, and MIT physREFS.
